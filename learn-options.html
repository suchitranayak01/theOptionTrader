A. Buying Strategies

  • Long Call: Buy calls when you expect the underlying stock to rise significantly. Best during bullish trends or before positive events.
  • Long Put: Buy puts when you expect the stock to fall. Ideal for hedging or profiting from bearish moves.
  • Bull Call Spread: Buy a call at lower strike and sell at higher strike to reduce cost while limiting profit.
  • Bear Put Spread: Buy a put at higher strike and sell at lower strike for defined risk/reward in bearish markets.
  • Long Straddle: Buy both call and put at the same strike when expecting high volatility but uncertain of direction.
  • Long Strangle: Buy OTM call and put when expecting large moves with less premium outlay than straddle.

ðŸ’¡ Pro Tip: Risk Management

Never risk more than 2-3% of your trading capital on a single options trade. Options can expire worthless, so always have an exit strategy.

B. When to Buy Options - Detailed Guide

  • Before Major Events: Earnings announcements, RBI policy meetings, budget releases, and corporate actions can create significant price movements.
  • During Trends: Buy calls in strong uptrends with increasing volume. Buy puts in downtrends with distribution patterns.
  • Low Implied Volatility: Options are cheaper when IV is low. Historical IV percentile below 30% indicates good buying opportunity.
  • Technical Breakouts: When stock breaks resistance (for calls) or support (for puts) with strong volume.
  • Time Considerations: Buy options with at least 30-45 days to expiry to avoid rapid theta decay. Weekly options only for very short-term plays.
  • Seasonal Patterns: Some stocks have seasonal trends (e.g., FMCG stocks before festivals, auto stocks during festive season).

Option selling is a probabilistic game where you earn premiums by selling time decay. Professional sellers understand market structure, volatility cycles, and risk management.

A. Selling Strategies

  • Covered Call: Sell calls against stock holdings to generate income in sideways markets.
  • Cash-Secured Put: Sell puts to potentially buy stocks at lower prices while earning premium.
  • Iron Condor: Sell OTM call spread and put spread simultaneously in range-bound markets.
  • Iron Butterfly: Sell ATM straddle and buy OTM strangle for defined risk premium collection.
  • Credit Spreads: Vertical spreads with net credit - bull put spread or bear call spread.
  • Ratio Spreads: Sell more options than bought to maximize premium collection.
  • Calendar Spreads: Sell near-term options and buy longer-term to profit from time decay differential.

B. What Impacts Options Premiums

  • Implied Volatility (IV): Higher IV = Higher premiums. IV increases before events and during uncertainty.
  • Time Decay (Theta): Premiums decay faster in last 30 days. Theta acceleration benefits sellers.
  • Underlying Price Movement: Delta impact - premiums increase as strikes get closer to ITM.
  • Interest Rates: Higher rates slightly increase call premiums and decrease put premiums.
  • Dividends: Upcoming dividends increase put premiums and decrease call premiums.
  • Supply & Demand: Heavy buying increases premiums, heavy selling decreases them.

C. Events That Impact Premiums

  • Earnings Announcements - IV spike 3-5 days before, crush after
  • RBI Monetary Policy - affects Nifty and Bank Nifty significantly
  • Budget Announcements - sector-specific volatility increases
  • Global Market Events - Fed meetings, geopolitical tensions
  • Expiry Day Effects - max pain theory, pin risk
  • Quarterly Results Season - broad market IV expansion

D. When to Sell Options

  • High IV Environment: Sell when IV percentile is above 50-70%
  • Post-Event: Immediately after earnings/events when IV crashes
  • Range-Bound Markets: Sell strangles/iron condors when stock is in consolidation
  • Strong Support/Resistance: Sell puts at support, calls at resistance
  • 30-45 DTE: Optimal time to sell for balance between premium and time
  • High PCR Readings: Sell puts when PCR >1.3, calls when PCR <0.7

E. Create Your Own Strategy

Strategy Builder Framework

  • Step 1: Define market outlook (bullish, bearish, neutral)
  • Step 2: Assess volatility environment (expanding or contracting)
  • Step 3: Choose base strategy (directional, non-directional, volatility)
  • Step 4: Optimize strike selection based on probability
  • Step 5: Set position sizing (max 5% portfolio per trade)
  • Step 6: Define entry rules (technical triggers, IV levels)
  • Step 7: Set exit criteria (profit target 50-70%, stop loss at 2x credit)
  • Step 8: Plan adjustment triggers (if underlying moves 10-15%)
  • Step 9: Backtest on historical data
  • Step 10: Paper trade for 20-30 occurrences before live trading

Advanced: Event-Based Selling

Sell options 1-2 days before major events when IV is at peak, then buy back post-event during IV crush. Requires quick execution and careful position sizing. Works best with weekly options on highly liquid instruments like Nifty and Bank Nifty.
